Radar PRF & Ambiguity

The pulse repetition frequency of a pulsed radar sets a fundamental trade: a low PRF gives long unambiguous range but a low blind speed, while a high PRF does the reverse. This tool returns the maximum unambiguous range and velocity, the first blind speed, the PRI and duty cycle, and the range resolution from the pulse width — the numbers behind choosing a low-, medium- or high-PRF waveform.

Equations & Parameters ▸
\(\text{PRI}=\dfrac{1}{\text{PRF}} \qquad R_{ua}=\dfrac{c}{2\,\text{PRF}} \qquad v_{ua}=\pm\dfrac{\lambda\,\text{PRF}}{4}\)
\(v_{blind}=\dfrac{n\,\lambda\,\text{PRF}}{2} \qquad d=\dfrac{\tau}{\text{PRI}}=\tau\cdot\text{PRF} \qquad \Delta R=\dfrac{c\,\tau}{2}\)
PRFPulse repetition frequency (kHz).
f0Carrier frequency (GHz), for the velocity ambiguity.
τPulse width (µs), optional — gives duty cycle and range resolution.
RuaMaximum unambiguous range — beyond it, echoes fold into the next PRI.
vblindBlind speeds where the Doppler shift is a multiple of the PRF (n = 1, 2, …).
Reference: M. I. Skolnik, Introduction to Radar Systems, 3rd ed., McGraw-Hill, 2001.
