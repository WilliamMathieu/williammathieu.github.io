LC Filter Designer (Butterworth / Chebyshev)

LC ladder filters are lossless passive filters built from alternating series inductors and shunt capacitors (or vice versa). Butterworth filters have the flattest possible passband (maximally flat) at the cost of a gradual roll-off. Chebyshev filters allow ripple in the passband in exchange for a much steeper roll-off outside the passband. Both are specified by order n — higher order gives steeper roll-off.

Filter typeButterworth: flat passband, moderate roll-off. Chebyshev: ripple in passband, steep roll-off.
ResponseLow-pass: passes below fc. High-pass: passes above fc.
nFilter order. Each additional order adds one LC element and ~20 dB/decade additional roll-off.
fcCutoff (−3 dB) frequency for Butterworth; edge-of-ripple frequency for Chebyshev.
R₀Source and load impedance (Ω). Standard: 50 Ω.
RippleChebyshev only: peak-to-peak ripple in the passband (dB). Typical: 0.1–1 dB.
