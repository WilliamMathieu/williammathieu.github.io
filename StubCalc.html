Transmission Line Stub Calculator

A stub is a shunt (parallel) section of transmission line used for impedance matching. By choosing its length and termination (short/open), it presents a target susceptance to cancel the load susceptance — single-stub matching.

fOperating frequency.
Z₀Characteristic impedance of the stub (Ω).
BTarget shunt susceptance (mS). Imaginary part of the admittance the stub must present.
Stub typeShort-circuit: lower loss. Open-circuit: easier on PCBs (no via needed).
Electrical lengthStub length in degrees at f.
Physical lengthStub length in mm (free-space). Multiply by √εeff for microstrip.
