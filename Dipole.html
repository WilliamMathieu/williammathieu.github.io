Dipole Antenna Calculator

A half-wave dipole is the most fundamental antenna. Its total length is approximately half a wavelength, with each arm being a quarter wavelength. In practice, the resonant length is slightly shorter than λ/2 due to the finite diameter of the conductor — captured here by the velocity factor k (typically 0.93–0.97 for thin wire). The dipole has an omnidirectional radiation pattern in the plane perpendicular to its axis and a gain of approximately 2.15 dBi.
