MRI SAR Estimator

Specific Absorption Rate (SAR) measures power deposited per unit mass in tissue (W/kg). IEC 60601-2-33 limits whole-body SAR to 2 W/kg (normal mode) and local head SAR to 3.2 W/kg. SAR scales with B₁⁺ amplitude squared, frequency squared, and tissue conductivity.

Parameter reference ▸
B₁⁺RF excitation field amplitude at the tissue (µT). Typical body coil: 10–20 µT for a 90° pulse.
fLarmor frequency. SAR scales as f² (or B₀²).
σTissue electrical conductivity (S/m). Muscle ≈ 0.7–0.9 S/m at 128 MHz.
ρTissue mass density (kg/m³). Muscle ≈ 1050 kg/m³.
Duty cycleFraction of time RF is on. SAR is time-averaged.
SAR formulaSAR = σ·|E|²/(2ρ) ≈ (ω·µ₀·B₁⁺)²·σ/(2ρ·µ₀²) = ω²·(B₁⁺)²·σ/(2ρ).
