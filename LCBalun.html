LC BALUN Calculator

A BALUN (BALanced-UNbalanced) converts between a single-ended (unbalanced) transmission line and a balanced (differential) connection. An LC BALUN uses a combination of inductors and capacitors to achieve this conversion while simultaneously transforming impedance. It is widely used in RF applications to connect coaxial cables to dipole antennas, differential amplifiers, and balanced mixers.

This calculator covers three common LC BALUN topologies:

Lattice / Bridged-TUses two inductors and two capacitors in a bridge configuration. Provides 90° phase split between balanced ports. True broadband behaviour.
LC π-BALUNTwo shunt capacitors and a series inductor. Low-pass structure. Simple and compact — common in RFIC and board-level designs.
Marchand BALUNTwo coupled quarter-wave resonators. True wideband performance. This calculator gives the equivalent lumped-element LC approximation valid near the design frequency.
