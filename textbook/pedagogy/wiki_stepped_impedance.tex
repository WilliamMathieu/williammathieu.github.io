\begin{workedexample}[Worked Example: Hi-Z / lo-Z sections]
A stepped-impedance low-pass alternates short high-impedance lines (series
inductors) and low-impedance lines (shunt capacitors). A short line of length
$\ell$ contributes
\[ L \approx \frac{Z_h\,\ell}{v_p}, \qquad C \approx \frac{\ell}{Z_l\,v_p}, \]
so a higher $Z_h$ raises the equivalent series $L$ and a lower $Z_l$ raises the
shunt $C$. The pattern approximates an LC ladder in microstrip with no lumped
parts.
\end{workedexample}

\begin{keytakeaways}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=2pt]
\item High-$Z$ (narrow) lines emulate series inductors; low-$Z$ (wide) lines emulate shunt capacitors.
\item It builds an LC low-pass from microstrip alone --- easy to fabricate.
\item Accuracy falls near and above cutoff where the lumped approximation breaks down.
\end{itemize}
\end{keytakeaways}

\begin{exercises}
\item A narrow, high-$Z$ microstrip section behaves as which element?
\item A wide, low-$Z$ section behaves as which element?
\item To increase the emulated series inductance, raise or lower the section's $Z_0$?
\end{exercises}
