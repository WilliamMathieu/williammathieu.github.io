\begin{workedexample}[Worked Example: Normalized impedance and reflection]
A load $Z_L = 75 + j50\ \Omega$ in a $50\ \Omega$ system normalizes to
$z = Z_L/Z_0 = 1.5 + j1.0$. Its reflection coefficient is
\[ \Gamma = \frac{z-1}{z+1} = \frac{0.5 + j1.0}{2.5 + j1.0} = 0.38\angle 39^{\circ}, \]
so $|\Gamma| = 0.38$ (VSWR $\approx 2.2$). On the chart the point lies in the
upper, inductive half, on the $r = 1.5$ resistance circle.
\end{workedexample}

\begin{keytakeaways}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=2pt]
\item The chart plots $\Gamma$; constant-$r$ circles and constant-$x$ arcs overlay it.
\item A $\lambda/4$ line (a $180^{\circ}$ trip in $\Gamma$) turns $z$ into its admittance $y = 1/z$.
\item Motion toward the generator is clockwise; one full revolution is $\lambda/2$.
\end{itemize}
\end{keytakeaways}

\begin{exercises}
\item Normalize $Z_L = 25 - j50\ \Omega$ in a $50\ \Omega$ system.
\item Where on the chart are a short circuit and an open circuit?
\item A load lies on the $r = 1$ circle. What does that imply for matching with a series reactance?
\end{exercises}
