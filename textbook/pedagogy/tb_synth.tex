\begin{workedexample}[Worked Example: DDS resolution]
A direct digital synthesizer has an $n = 32$-bit phase accumulator clocked
at $f_{clk} = 100\ \text{MHz}$. Its frequency resolution is
\[ \Delta f = \frac{f_{clk}}{2^{\,32}} = \frac{10^{8}}{4.29\times10^{9}} = 0.023\ \text{Hz}, \]
and a tuning word $M = 2^{30}$ produces
$f_{out} = (2^{30}/2^{32})\,f_{clk} = 0.25\times100 = 25\ \text{MHz}$. DDS trades
this fine, fast control against DAC spurs and a Nyquist limit of $f_{clk}/2$.
\end{workedexample}

\begin{keytakeaways}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=2pt]
\item Charge-pump type-II PLL; design around loop bandwidth $\omega_c$ and $45$--$60^\circ$ phase margin.
\item Wide loop: fast, low VCO noise, more spur; narrow loop: clean but slow.
\item Fractional-N decouples step from $f_{ref}$; DDS: $f_{out}=(M/2^n)f_{clk}$, resolution $f_{clk}/2^n$.
\end{itemize}
\end{keytakeaways}
