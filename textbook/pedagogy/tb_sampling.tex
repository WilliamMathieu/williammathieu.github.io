\begin{workedexample}[Worked Example: ADC SNR and the jitter limit]
A $12$-bit ADC has an ideal, quantization-limited SNR of
\[ \text{SNR} = 6.02(12) + 1.76 = 74\ \text{dB}. \]
But sampling a $f_{in} = 100\ \text{MHz}$ input with $\sigma_t = 1\ \text{ps}$ of
clock jitter caps the SNR at
\[ -20\log_{10}(2\pi f_{in}\sigma_t) = -20\log_{10}(2\pi\cdot10^{8}\cdot10^{-12}) = 64\ \text{dB}. \]
Here jitter, not quantization, limits performance --- a common situation for
high-IF sampling.
\end{workedexample}

\begin{keytakeaways}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=2pt]
\item Energy above $f_s/2$ aliases into the first Nyquist zone; filter before the ADC.
\item Bandpass sampling needs only $f_s \ge 2B$, folding a high band into a Nyquist zone.
\item ADC SNR $= 6.02N + 1.76\ \text{dB}$; jitter caps it at $-20\log_{10}(2\pi f_{in}\sigma_t)$.
\end{itemize}
\end{keytakeaways}
