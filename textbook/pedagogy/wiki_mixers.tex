\begin{workedexample}[Worked Example: Downconversion and the image]
A mixer multiplies RF and LO, producing outputs at $f_{LO} \pm f_{RF}$. For
$f_{RF} = 2000\ \text{MHz}$ and $f_{LO} = 1800\ \text{MHz}$, the IF is
$|f_{RF} - f_{LO}| = 200\ \text{MHz}$. The \emph{image} at
$f_{LO} - f_{IF} = 1600\ \text{MHz}$ also lands at that IF and must be rejected
by filtering ahead of the mixer.
\end{workedexample}

\begin{keytakeaways}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=2pt]
\item A mixer produces $f_{LO} \pm f_{RF}$; the wanted IF is the sum or difference.
\item The image ($f_{LO} \mp f_{IF}$) maps onto the same IF and must be filtered or rejected.
\item Passive diode mixers have conversion loss; active mixers can have gain but add noise.
\end{itemize}
\end{keytakeaways}

\begin{exercises}
\item $f_{RF} = 900\ \text{MHz}$, $f_{LO} = 800\ \text{MHz}$. What is the IF?
\item For that low-side-LO case, where is the image?
\item What blocks the image before it reaches the mixer?
\end{exercises}
