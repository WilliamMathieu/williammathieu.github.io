\begin{workedexample}[Worked Example: Effective permittivity of microstrip]
Microstrip fields are partly in air and partly in the substrate, so the
effective permittivity lies between 1 and $\varepsilon_r$. A common estimate is
\[ \varepsilon_{\text{eff}} \approx \frac{\varepsilon_r + 1}{2}
   + \frac{\varepsilon_r - 1}{2}\,\frac{1}{\sqrt{1 + 12h/W}}. \]
On FR-4 ($\varepsilon_r = 4.4$) with $W/h = 2$:
$\varepsilon_{\text{eff}} = 2.7 + 1.7/\sqrt{7} = 3.34$. The guided wavelength is
then $\lambda_g = \lambda_0/\sqrt{\varepsilon_{\text{eff}}}$.
\end{workedexample}

\begin{keytakeaways}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=2pt]
\item Microstrip's mixed air/dielectric fields give an \emph{effective} permittivity between 1 and $\varepsilon_r$.
\item Stripline is fully enclosed (TEM, non-dispersive); CPW has coplanar grounds; coax is shielded and broadband.
\item Wider lines (larger $W/h$) give lower $Z_0$.
\end{itemize}
\end{keytakeaways}

\begin{exercises}
\item A stripline sits in a uniform $\varepsilon_r = 4$ dielectric. What is its effective permittivity?
\item Does widening a microstrip raise or lower $Z_0$?
\item Find the guided wavelength at $2\ \text{GHz}$ for $\varepsilon_{\text{eff}} = 3.34$.
\end{exercises}
