\begin{workedexample}[Worked Example: Polarization-mismatch loss]
Two linearly polarized antennas misaligned by angle $\theta$ couple power
as $\cos^2\theta$. For $\theta = 45^{\circ}$,
$\text{loss} = -10\log_{10}(\cos^2 45^{\circ}) = -10\log_{10}(0.5) = 3\ \text{dB}$.
Cross-polarized ($90^{\circ}$) antennas couple $\cos^2 90^{\circ} = 0$ (infinite
loss in theory), while linear-to-ideal-circular always loses $3\ \text{dB}$
regardless of rotation.
\end{workedexample}

\begin{keytakeaways}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=2pt]
\item Linear-to-linear mismatch loss $= -10\log_{10}(\cos^2\theta)$; $90^{\circ}$ gives (ideally) no coupling.
\item Linear-to-circular is a fixed $3\ \text{dB}$ loss.
\item Axial ratio (dB) measures circularity; $0\ \text{dB}$ is perfectly circular.
\end{itemize}
\end{keytakeaways}

\item Loss between a linear and an ideal circular antenna?
\item Axial ratio of a perfectly circular wave?
\end{exercises}
