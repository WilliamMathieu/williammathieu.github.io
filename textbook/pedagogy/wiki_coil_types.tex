\begin{workedexample}[Worked Example: Surface versus volume coil]
A surface coil sits close to the anatomy, sees little sample noise, and
gives high SNR near the surface --- but its sensitivity falls off roughly as
$1/d^3$ with depth and its $B_1$ is inhomogeneous. A volume coil (e.g.\ a
birdcage) surrounds the region for uniform $B_1$ and coverage at lower peak SNR.
Phased arrays combine many small elements to get surface-coil SNR over a
volume-coil field of view.
\end{workedexample}

\begin{keytakeaways}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=2pt]
\item Surface coils: high local SNR, poor depth penetration and uniformity.
\item Volume coils (birdcage): uniform $B_1$ and coverage, lower peak SNR.
\item Phased arrays merge both: many small elements $\to$ high SNR over a large FOV.
\end{itemize}
\end{keytakeaways}

\begin{exercises}
\item Which gives higher SNR near the skin: surface or volume coil?
\item Which gives more uniform $B_1$?
\item What coil type gets surface-coil SNR over a large FOV?
\end{exercises}
