\begin{workedexample}[Worked Example: Directional coupler tap]
A directional coupler samples a fixed fraction of the forward wave. A
``$20\ \text{dB}$ coupler'' delivers $P_{\text{coupled}} = P_{in} - 20\ \text{dB}$,
i.e.\ $1\%$ of the input power to the coupled port. Its \emph{directivity} --- the
ratio of forward to reverse coupling --- should exceed $20\ \text{dB}$ so the
coupled port samples mainly the forward wave.
\end{workedexample}

\begin{keytakeaways}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=2pt]
\item A directional coupler samples the forward (or reverse) wave; coupling in dB sets the tap fraction.
\item Directivity measures how well forward and reverse are separated; higher is better.
\item A circulator routes power one way port-to-port; terminate one port to make an isolator.
\end{itemize}
\end{keytakeaways}
