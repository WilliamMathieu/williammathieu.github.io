\begin{workedexample}[Worked Example: Nearest E12 value]
The E12 series has 12 values per decade, spaced by $10^{1/12} = 1.21$
($21\%$ steps, matching $\pm10\%$ parts). Suppose a design needs
$R = 2.6\ \text{k}\Omega$. The E12 neighbours are $2.2$ and $2.7\ \text{k}\Omega$;
$2.7\ \text{k}\Omega$ is closest ($3.8\%$ away, inside the $10\%$ band), so it is
the value to fit.
\end{workedexample}

\begin{keytakeaways}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=2pt]
\item E$n$ has $n$ values per decade, spaced by $10^{1/n}$; larger $n$ means tighter steps.
\item E6/E12/E24 pair with $20/10/5\%$ tolerances; E96 is the $1\%$ series.
\item Round a computed value to the nearest series member within tolerance.
\end{itemize}
\end{keytakeaways}
