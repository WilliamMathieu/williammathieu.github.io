Smith Chart

The Smith Chart is a graphical tool for visualising complex impedances and reflection coefficients. Any point on the chart represents an impedance Z = R + jX, normalised to Z₀. The horizontal axis is the real axis (pure resistance); moving clockwise around the chart corresponds to moving toward the generator along a transmission line. The outer boundary is |Γ| = 1 (total reflection). Points can be plotted by clicking the chart or entering R and X above.

Z₀Reference impedance for normalisation (typically 50 Ω).
RResistance (Ω) — real part of the impedance.
XReactance (Ω) — imaginary part. Positive = inductive, negative = capacitive.
|Γ|Magnitude of the reflection coefficient. 0 = perfect match, 1 = total reflection.
Return Loss-20·log₁₀(|Γ|) in dB. Higher is better — >10 dB is typically acceptable.
VSWRVoltage Standing Wave Ratio = (1+|Γ|)/(1−|Γ|). VSWR = 1 is a perfect match.

Click anywhere on the chart to read off impedance. Or enter R+jX above to plot a point.
