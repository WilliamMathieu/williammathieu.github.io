dBm is a logarithmic power unit referenced to 1 milliwatt. It is the standard unit for RF power levels because the large dynamic range of RF systems (from picowatts to kilowatts) is unwieldy in linear units. dB (without a reference) expresses a ratio — a gain or loss. Power ratios use 10·log₁₀, while voltage or field amplitude ratios use 20·log₁₀ (because power ∝ voltage²).

dBmPower level in dBm = 10·log₁₀(P/1mW). 0 dBm = 1 mW, 30 dBm = 1 W, −30 dBm = 1 µW.
W / mW / µWLinear power. RF receivers often operate at −90 to −120 dBm; transmitters at +20 to +50 dBm.
dB (power ratio)Gain or loss in dB = 10·log₁₀(P_out/P_in). 3 dB ≈ factor of 2, 10 dB = factor of 10.
dB (voltage ratio)Voltage or field amplitude ratio in dB = 20·log₁₀(V_out/V_in). 6 dB ≈ factor of 2.
