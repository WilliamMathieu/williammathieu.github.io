Ferrite Gyromagnetic Resonance

A biased ferrite has electron spins that precess about the DC field at the Larmor frequency f₀ = γH₀ (about 2.8 MHz per oersted). Near this frequency the material's permeability becomes a tensor — the Polder tensor with diagonal element μ and off-diagonal element κ — which is what makes non-reciprocal devices (isolators, circulators, phase shifters) work. Sample shape shifts the resonance through demagnetisation (the Kittel equation). This tool computes f₀, the saturation frequency fm, the shape-dependent resonance, and μ, κ at your operating frequency.
